## Overview

When several keywords match different words of a compound, the longest wins even when it's a
modifier — "Ginger beer" → 🥔, "Carrot cake" → 🥕. Prefer the head noun (rightmost word).

## Do

In the simple-keyword matcher emitted by `tools/gen_emoji.py` into `EmojiTable.swift`: when
multiple simple keywords match DIFFERENT words of a multi-word name, prefer the match on the
RIGHTMOST word of the name (English compounds are head-final), using keyword length only as the
tie-break within the same word. Complex (multi-word) keyword matches keep absolute priority over
simple matches, as today. Regenerate via the generator — no hand edits to the table.
Verified fixes this buys with no new data: "ginger beer" → the `beer` match (🍺) instead of
`ginger`; "carrot cake" → `cake` (🎂) not 🥕; "mince pies" → `pie` (🥧) not 🥩; "assam tea" →
`tea` (🍵); "tuna in spring water" → still needs care: `tuna` should win over `water` — words
like "in"/"of" are stopwords, but `tuna` is not the rightmost content word. To handle that class,
scan content words right-to-left but fall back to the longest match anywhere if the rightmost
content word has no keyword match.
