# Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 3 July 2026_

Basket is a shopping list app built to work entirely on your device — nothing
about how you use it is collected, transmitted, or seen by anyone else,
including the developer.

## What Basket does with your data

Everything you enter into Basket — your list, item names, quantities, and the
history the app uses to suggest items as you type — is stored locally on your
device using Apple's SwiftData framework. None of it is sent anywhere. Basket
has no servers, no analytics, and no account or login of any kind.

## Third parties

Basket doesn't use any third-party SDKs, ad networks, or trackers. No data is
shared with anyone, because no data leaves your device in the first place.

## In-app purchases (the tip jar)

Basket includes an optional tip jar (three small in-app purchases). These are
processed entirely by Apple through StoreKit — Basket never sees, stores, or
has access to your payment details. Apple's own privacy policy governs that
transaction; you can read it at <https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/>.

The only thing Basket remembers about a tip is a single yes/no flag ("has this
device ever tipped?"), stored locally, so the app can say a quiet thank-you.
That flag never leaves your device either.

## Children's privacy

Basket doesn't collect data from anyone, of any age, so there's nothing
further to disclose here beyond the above.

## Changes to this policy

If this ever changes, it'll be reflected on this page, with an updated date
at the top.
